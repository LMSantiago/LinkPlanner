\mysection{Quantum Key Distribution}\large
\vspace*{0.5cm}
Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) is a secure way of sharing a unique random key (composed of 0 and 1) between two parties spatially distant. They later use this random key to encrypt and decrypt messages between them.

The two main types of QKD are Polirization protocols and Time Bin protocols.

The Coherent One Way (COW) protocol, uses the Time Bin properties.

\mysection{COW - Protocol}\large
\vspace*{0.3cm}

\begin{description}
  \item[Step 1] Alice creates a random key using:
      $$|0\rangle = |\alpha\rangle |0\rangle$$
      $$|1\rangle = |0\rangle |\alpha\rangle$$
      $$|d\rangle = |\alpha\rangle |\alpha\rangle$$

      where $|0\rangle$ is the vacuum state and $|\alpha\rangle$ is a coherent state of light with intensity $\mu=|\alpha|^2$ and spreads a few random decoy states ($|d\rangle$) in random locations during the creation of the key.
      
  \begin{figure}[hbt]
    	\centering

        	\label{Simple}
    \end{figure}
      
      $$|...0d10...\rangle = |...:0\alpha:\alpha\alpha:\alpha0:0\alpha...\rangle$$

\end{description}

\mysection{COW - Protocol}\large
\vspace*{1cm}
\begin{description}


\item [Step 3]Alice informs Bob when she sent a decoy pulse.

\item [Step 4]They calculate the visibility (V) and the QBER (Q) of the key.
$$ V = \frac{I_{max}-I_{min}}{I_{max}+I_{min}}$$
where the $I_{max}$ and $I_{min}$ are the average pulse intensities for constructive and destructive interference respectively.

They also share a small part of the key in a public channel, to see if there are errors in the message.
\end{description}
\vspace*{1cm}
A loss of coherence and therefore a reduction of the visibility 
reveal the presence of an eavesdropper, in which case the key is simply discarded
